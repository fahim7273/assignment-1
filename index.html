The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Mohammad Yunus, an internationally renowned economist and social entrepreneur, served as the Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh during a transitional government period in 2007. However, his primary recognition comes from founding Grameen Bank, pioneering the concept of microfinance and microcredit, which has uplifted millions of impoverished people worldwide. Dr. Yunus received the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 jointly with Grameen Bank for their efforts to create economic and social development from below. His innovative approach to providing small loans without collateral transformed lives by enabling poor individuals, especially women, to start small businesses and achieve financial independence.
